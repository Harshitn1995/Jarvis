Preface: Different Users, Different Behaviour, Different Needs

The primary goal of any modern marketer is to create great customer experiences and build loyalty. This ties back to your users, their behavior, and how they interact with your product or brand.

Different users have different needs and respond to the same message in different ways. That’s why marketers adopt personalized and contextual lifecycle communication—giving users what they want, when they want it, on the right channel.

But managing expectations for millions of users requires continuous behavioral tracking and data-driven communication.

🔍 Understanding the User Lifecycle

To deliver contextual communication, marketers must understand the stages users go through when engaging with a product. Emotional states are hard to track—but behavioral states (actions) are not.

For example, actions like product search, filter application, viewing product details, and purchasing define a user’s journey and are measurable touchpoints.

By understanding the user lifecycle, you can design messaging strategies that are relevant at each stage.

📊 Why Behavioural Data Matters

  • Helps interpret user motivations and barriers to conversion.
  • Reveals missing touchpoints or features that hinder progress.
  • Forms the foundation for segmentation and personalization.

👍 Use-case

If users are stuck at the evaluation stage, interventions like offers, testimonials, UI enhancements, or social proof can motivate them to convert.

📌 Behavioural Data in Jarvis

In Jarvis, behavioral data is captured as Events. These represent every significant user action on your app or site.

Each event includes Attributes—time, device, location, value, and more—offering detailed context.

This data helps you build accurate segments, trigger personalized campaigns, and evaluate user engagement strategies effectively.
